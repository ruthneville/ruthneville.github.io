About

I am a spatial data scientist specialising in population geography and demography, with a focus on housing and migration and mobility. My PhD research investigated international student mobility and the socio-economic, cultural, economic, and institutional factors shaping migration flows, with an emphasis on the impact of external shocks such as Brexit and the COVID-19 pandemic. My work employs advanced methodologies, including machine learning forecasting models and spatial interaction models to predict and measure population trends. I have also contributed to projects that leverage innovative spatial data sources, such as digital trace data, to analyse population movements during crises. I am currently employed as a Research Fellow on the No Space Like Home project, understanding the spatial distribution of small homes in England.

PhD Research: Drivers, Disruptions, and Future Trajectories: Understanding International Student Mobility to the UK

Completion Date: July 2025

My PhD research explores the trends, disruptions, and future pathways of international students coming to the UK ‚Äî a country that attracts more overseas students than almost anywhere else in the world.

Key Areas of Analysis:

  • Drivers: What drives students to choose the UK ‚Äî from economic and cultural ties to language and development levels.
  • Disruptions: How global events reshape mobility ‚Äî including the impacts of Brexit and the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Future Trajectories: Where student flows are headed next ‚Äî using advanced forecasting methods that blend statistical modelling and machine learning.

Key Findings:

  • A non-linear link between development and mobility, challenging ‚Äúone-size-fits-all‚Äù migration models.
  • A sharp post-Brexit drop in EU applications (especially from Eastern Europe), alongside pandemic-related increases from certain countries.
  • Projections to 2029 suggesting growth from India, continued EU declines, and possible stagnation from China.

By combining spatial analysis, difference-in-differences, and forecasting, this work offers new insights for policy and planning ‚Äî helping ensure the UK remains a competitive study destination in an uncertain global landscape.
